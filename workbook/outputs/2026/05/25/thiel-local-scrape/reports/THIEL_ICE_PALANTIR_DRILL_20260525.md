## Guardrail

- Do not write "Thiel personally ordered deportations." The record supports chairman/co-founder accountability, not operational command.
- Do not write "Palantir decides who ICE arrests." ICE officials and DHS documents frame Palantir tools as lead, data, case-management, tip-processing, targeting-support, and logistics infrastructure.
- Do write "Palantir software supports ICE/HSI investigative case management and enforcement operations."
- Do separate HSI criminal-investigation framing from ERO deportation/removal framing. The public record shows overlap and expansion, but the specific system/function matters.
- Do not rely on rhetoric alone. The power of this lane is the contract/system stack.

5. A 2026 DHS-wide Palantir purchasing agreement that makes it easier for DHS components, including ICE, to buy Palantir products.

### ICM as the backbone

DHS describes ICM as a major ICE IT system and the core law-enforcement case-management tool for HSI agents and personnel. FedScoop reported in 2022 that the renewed Palantir contract was worth about $95.5M over five years and that ICM is DHS's system of record across HSI investigative cases.

Use:

- "The key is not a single raid. The key is the system of record."
- "The moral lane is centralized case-building, not just one field operation."

### Deportation use and the HSI/ERO dodge

The standard defense is: HSI investigates crime; ERO handles deportation. The public record makes that distinction important, but not exculpatory. TechCrunch reported in 2019 that documents showed Palantir software used in a 2017 deportation-related operation. ACLU's 2026 roundup argues ICM has become part of a broader removal/deportation apparatus and is linked to data consolidation across public and private sources.

Use:

- "The defense says case management; the documents show deportation workflows touching the case-management stack."
- "Even if the tool is framed as HSI case management, the same data architecture can feed removal operations."

Do not overstate:

- Do not say every ICM use is deportation.
- Do not say HSI and ERO are identical.

### ImmigrationOS

The 2025 ImmigrationOS lane is the escalation point. Public summaries of ICE contract documents say ICE sought a prototype before Sept. 25, 2025, under the existing ICM task order. The described functions include selecting/prioritizing people for apprehension, tracking self-deportations and visa overstays with near-real-time visibility, and improving logistics from identification to removal.

Use:

- "ICM is the record system. ImmigrationOS is the deportation-workflow layer."
- "This is no longer only a database story. It is a lifecycle-management story."
- "The phrase to drill is 'from identification to removal.'"

Do not overstate:

- Pull the raw LSJ/PDF before using exact contract-language claims in a published piece.
- Distinguish prototype, task order, and full production status.

### AI tip processing and ELITE

Wired reported in January 2026 that DHS's 2025 AI inventory disclosed AI-Enhanced ICE Tip Processing using Palantir generative AI tools to summarize/categorize tips and translate non-English submissions. Wired also reported on ELITE, a Palantir-developed tool for enforcement targeting support.

FedScoop reported January 2026 that ICE added 25 AI use cases after July 2025, including Palantir products for tip processing, ELITE, and software development. It also reported risk-governance concerns around use cases that were presumed high-impact but deemed not high-impact.

FedScoop also reported Elliston said Palantir powers ICE's current targeting system and associated it with an 80% success rate, meaning four out of five times agents go to a location of interest, they leave with someone in handcuffs.
