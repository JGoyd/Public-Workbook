# Zorro Ranch Truth Commission Brief

Prepared: 2026-05-20

Purpose: This brief summarizes the Zorro Ranch evidence lane for a New Mexico truth-commission-style inquiry. It separates what the corpus supports from what remains unproven, and it identifies the records and witnesses most likely to cross the remaining legal thresholds.

## Executive Summary

Zorro Ranch is an actionable public-interest inquiry because the records show post-conviction guest access, staff-managed logistics, operational record systems, ranch facilities, aviation access, telecom/security infrastructure, and a New Mexico sex-offender-registration question that was legally salient in later litigation.

The current record supports subpoenas and witness interviews. It does not yet prove a New Mexico registration violation, spoliation, abuse at Zorro, or misconduct by named guests.

The correct framing is:

> Zorro Ranch appears to have generated records that can test who went there, when they went, where they stayed, who arranged it, whether Epstein was physically present, how long he stayed, and whether any New Mexico registration or reporting obligation was triggered.

## Finding 4: New Mexico Registration Is The Sharpest Legal Theory, But It Is Not Proven

Documents:

- `EFTA00074029`
- `EFTA00180921`
- `EFTA00184224`

Supported facts:

- Epstein's New Mexico sex-offender registration status was legally salient in later litigation.
- `EFTA00074029` states defense counsel submitted information regarding Epstein's New Mexico sex-offender registration status.
- The filing references an August 19, 2010 New Mexico Department of Public Safety letter stating Epstein was not required to register with New Mexico at that time for the 2008 Florida conviction.

Truth Commission value:

- New Mexico registration/residence/presence should be investigated directly through government records;
- the question is not ownership alone, but whether Epstein's actual presence, duration, temporary lodging, residence, employment, or travel notice duties triggered a reporting obligation.

Facts needed before alleging a violation:

- applicable New Mexico statute and trigger by year;
- exact physical presence dates at Zorro;
- overnight stays and duration;
- whether visitor, temporary-lodging, residence, employment, or school provisions applied;
- whether any origin jurisdiction required notice for New Mexico travel;
- NM DPS and county sheriff correspondence, determinations, and registration/non-registration records.

Current status:

- investigation justified;
- violation not proven.

## Final Position

Zorro Ranch should be treated as a records-and-witness inquiry with a specific legal target: physical presence and duration sufficient to test New Mexico registration/reporting obligations.

The present record crosses the subpoena floor. It does not cross the violation floor.
