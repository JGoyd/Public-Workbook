The Sequence in One Paragraph

In July 2017, David Stern — Jeffrey Epstein's Hong Kong intermediary — contacted Standard Chartered Group CEO Bill Winters to arrange a 5-day London work placement for the 16-year-old son of Norway's UK Ambassador. Winters explicitly identified the pattern as a known regulatory risk for internship-as-bribery and declined to proceed outside formal HR channels. Stern forwarded Winters' refusal to Epstein on the same day. The request then moved to Bank of China (UK) Limited, which is alleged to have accepted the candidate. The candidate — Edward Rød-Larsen — is identifiable as a Politically Exposed Person (PEP) under FCA Handbook definitions, triggering mandatory Enhanced Due Diligence that has not been documented.

Concurrent with the placement, Epstein's office paid medical invoices for Edward's father Terje Rød-Larsen (March 2018) and purchased Apple products for other family children (January 2019), transforming the arrangement from an isolated favour into a documented financial relationship. Separately, a 2011 email from Stern proposes an undeclared London front office for "European & China deals" where "nobody knows you [Epstein] are behind it," raising third-party due diligence concerns about Bank of China (UK)'s introducer controls. A supervisory query on these matters was filed with the FCA and referred by the FCA to the relevant supervisory team in May 2026.

This matter was reported to the FCA (ref 00Db00K8yP). The FCA confirmed receipt and referred the information to the BoC (UK) supervisory team on 13 May 2026. No finding or enforcement action has been announced.

The AML / Regulatory Concern

The candidate — Edward Rød-Larsen — is a PEP family member under FCA and JMLSG definitions: his mother was Norway's Ambassador to the UK; his father a former UN Under-Secretary-General. Any FCA-regulated firm entering a business relationship with a PEP or their close family member is required under the Money Laundering Regulations 2017 to conduct Enhanced Due Diligence, obtain senior management approval, and perform enhanced ongoing monitoring. None of these steps has been documented for BoC (UK)'s acceptance of the placement.

Concurrent with the placement, Epstein's office paid medical invoices for the candidate's father (March 2018) and purchased Apple products for other family children (January 2019). This documented financial relationship with the same PEP family, during the same window, is precisely the pattern that Bill Winters identified as a known regulatory risk when he declined the placement for Standard Chartered.

Why PEP Status Matters for BoC (UK)

Under the FCA Handbook and the Money Laundering Regulations 2017, a "Politically Exposed Person" includes family members and close associates of individuals who hold or have held prominent public functions — which encompasses both a serving ambassador and a former UN Under-Secretary-General. A bank entering into any business relationship (including an internship placement) with a PEP or their close family member must apply Enhanced Due Diligence, which requires establishing source of wealth, obtaining senior management approval for the relationship, and conducting enhanced ongoing monitoring. No such documentation has been identified for BoC (UK)'s alleged acceptance of this placement.
